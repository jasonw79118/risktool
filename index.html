Monte Carlo Method Explanation

This system uses Monte Carlo simulation to estimate risk exposure. Monte Carlo simulation is a quantitative risk analysis technique widely used in financial modeling, engineering, and regulatory risk management. The model performs thousands of randomized trials using defined probability ranges for uncertain inputs.

Inputs such as event impact and mitigation effectiveness are defined as ranges rather than fixed values. The simulation randomly samples values within those ranges and calculates resulting financial outcomes across many iterations.

The result is a probability distribution showing the range of potential outcomes and the most statistically likely impact.

Regulatory Alignment

Quantitative risk modeling techniques such as Monte Carlo simulation are commonly accepted within U.S. regulatory frameworks when used to evaluate uncertainty in financial exposure and operational risk.

The methodology implemented here:

  • Defines bounded ranges for uncertain variables
  • Uses randomized sampling across multiple iterations
  • Calculates probability distributions of outcomes
  • Produces expected loss estimates and confidence ranges
  • Documents assumptions used in the model

These characteristics support transparency and reproducibility, which are important considerations for regulatory review and internal model validation.
